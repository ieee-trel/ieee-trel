IEEE Transactions on Reliability

Special Section on AI and ML for Resilience Assessment and Enhancement of Cyber-Physical Systems


Call for Papers


Background


In the era of Industrial 4.0, Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) have become the core foundation in modern engineering systems due to their ubiquitous integration into physical infrastructure systems, online computing, and networking and control systems. For a CPS, resilience refers to the capacity of a system to recover from disruptions. Accurate resilience assessment and enhancement of CPSs are critical to ensure a reliable, stable and resilient CPS. To achieve this, the traditional empirical and data-driven modeling paradigm using human expertise and limited data to implement Prognostics and Health Management (PHM) and enhance the resilience of CPSs has become inadequate. In addition, as disturbances exist in the whole operating process, it�s very difficult, if not impossible, to realize assured resilient operations of CPSs using traditional methods. With the increasing adoption of digitalization of CPSs and availability of large quantity of data, Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) methods have shown the promising capability to learn the underlying operating mechanisms directly from a wide range of heterogenous data sets. Therefore, AI and ML methods render more sophisticated solutions to resolve the reliability and resilience challenges in CPSs.


The goal of this special section is to promote and collect AI and ML based research advancements including both methodological and practical research in modeling, prediction or control for resilience assessment and enhancement in CPSs. Submissions will be reviewed and selected based on innovation, practical relevance, technical rigor and presentation.

Topics


The topics of interest include, but are not limited to:


  • AI/ML methods for resilience assessment and enhancement
  • AI/ML methods for PHM
  • Degradation and resilience assessment for CPSs
  • Digital twin for reliability and maintenance of CPSs
  • Reinforcement learning for maintenance decision-making
  • Predictive/prescriptive maintenance policies for CPSs
  • AI/ML-based control, dispatching, planning for CPSs
  • AI and ML-supported prognostic control in CPSs
  • Resilience-oriented proactive engineering management using AI methods
  • Other relevant AI and ML approaches for PHM of CPSs

Submission Information


We welcome high-quality submissions that are original work, not published, and not currently submitted elsewhere. We also encourage extensions to conference papers, unless prohibited by copyright, if there is a significant difference in technical content. Improvements such as adding a new case study or including a description of additional related studies do not satisfy this requirement. A description explaining the differences between the conference paper and the journal submission is required. The overlap between each submission and other articles, including the authors� own papers and dissertations, should be less than 30%.


All submitted papers will undergo a rigorous peer-review process and must conform to the double-column, the single-spaced format of printed articles in the IEEE Transactions on Reliability with all figures and tables embedded in the paper, rather than listed at the end or in the appendix. Refer to the special guidelines posted at https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/tr-ieee.
